What are the goals for Artificial Intelligence?

Home Problems Goals Applications

There was no established theory that guildes AI research. The researchers disagreed about many issues and a few of the most long standing questions still remain unanswered. Those questions would be should artifical intelligence simulate natural intelligence by studying psychology or neurobiology? Can intelligent behavior be described using simple, elegant principles? Or does it necessarily require solving a large number of completely unrelated problems?

During the 1940s and 1950s a number of resesarcheres explored the connection between neurobiology, information theory and cybernetics. Of those researchers some built machines that used electronic networks to exhibit rudimentary intelligence. When digital computers became relevant in the 1950s the AI researchers explored the possibility that human intelligence could be reduced to symbol manipulation. The symbolic approaches had achieved great success at simulating high-level thinking in small demonstration programs.

The good old fashioned AI or GOFAI got bogged down on ad hoc patches to symbolic computation. This worked with the reseachers own toy models but failed to generalize to real-world results. Fast forwarding to 1990, AI researchers adopted sophisticated mathematical tools such as hidden markov models, information theory and decision theory. Statistical learning techniques were gaining higher levels of accuracy in many practical domains such as data mining. The increased successes with real-world data led to increasing emhasis on comparing different approaches against shared test data to see which approach performed best in a broader context.
